"Breaking Free: How Stepping Out of My Comfort Zone Shaped Who I Am"

If there is one thing I have learned in my life, it is that growth never happens inside a comfort zone. My name is Angel Cressencia Aletta, a sixth-semester student majoring in Information Systems at Telkom University Surabaya. My journey to becoming who I am today was not easy; it was shaped by fear, self-doubt, and the courage to push through both. Growing up, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic, I became deeply introverted. With social interactions reduced to almost nothing, I lost the confidence to connect with the world around me. Yet, it was precisely that struggle that ignited my desire to change, grow, and prove to myself that I am capable of more than I ever imagined.

The pandemic years left a mark on my social confidence that I did not fully realize until I returned to school in my final year of high school. Sitting in a classroom again, surrounded by classmates and teachers who still felt like strangers, I knew something had to change. I made a deliberate choice to re-enter the world of student organizations, even though every part of me was nervous. I was shy, unsure of myself, and afraid of judgment. But I refused to let those feelings define me. Taking that first step back into social life was uncomfortable, but it was also the most important decision I had ever made.

The real turning point came when one of my teachers offered me the opportunity to compete in a competition centered on social studies and public speaking—two things that terrified me the most. The moment I heard about it, a wave of fear washed over me. I was afraid of making mistakes, afraid of losing, and above all, afraid of embarrassing myself in front of others. Every insecurity I had screamed at me to say no. But I said yes. I trained hard, pushed past every fear, and stepped onto that stage with everything I had. To my own amazement I did not win just once—I won four times. Each of those four medals was not simply a trophy; it was proof that fear does not have to be the final answer.

Some may argue that introverts are better suited to quiet, independent environments and should not force themselves into social situations. While it is true that each person has their own natural temperament, I believe that introversion should never become an excuse to stop growing. There is a difference between respecting one's personality and letting it become a barrier. Challenging myself did not erase who I am—it expanded who I could become. The confidence I built through those competitions was not something I was born with—it was something I earned through discomfort and perseverance.

I carry those lessons with me into university life. I joined as a junior staff member of the Student Executive Board (BEM) to continue building meaningful connections, both within and beyond my campus community. The journey from a withdrawn, pandemic-era teenager to an active and engaged university student has taught me one enduring truth: never allow yourself to stay trapped in comfort. Force yourself to grow, silence the overthinking, and face what frightens you, because the future belongs to those who are willing to move forward, even when they are afraid.

THE IMPACT OF SOCIAL MEDIA STANDARDS ON SELF-ACCEPTANCE IN THE DIGITAL AGE

In this modern age, social media has become an essential part of people's lives. Social media serves not only as a means of communication but also has a significant influence on shaping social standards about their appearance, lifestyle, and success. This leads individuals to feel insecure and compare themselves to the unrealistic images they see on social media. However, some people argue that social media has a positive impact on self-acceptance, encourages personal growth and motivates individuals to become better versions of themselves. Despite some benefits, I believe that conforming to social media standards has a negative impact on self-acceptance, particularly on mental health, social relationship, and self-esteem. Therefore, this essay will analyze how social standards in the digital age influence an individual's self-perception and explore strategies to build self-acceptance in this era.

Social media standards in the digital age can encourage young people to improve themselves. Social media also has a positive impact as a source of inspiration and a way to earn income. Many kinds of content appear on the For Your Page (FYP), and these often become trends that give people ideas for creating content. This can motivate individuals to turn their ideas into positive and meaningful content. For example, trends about lifestyle or relationships can be used to create educational content. In addition, with the development of technology, social media can also be a source of income through affiliate marketing if someone is consistent in creating content. This shows that social media can support personal growth, provide inspiration and opportunities for self-expression, while also helping to improve economic conditions.

However, life is more than just comparing ourselves to social media standards. Social media standards can also make young people feel like they're never good enough. This happens because many young people feel pressured to keep up with the latest trends due to Fear of Missing Out (FOMO). They often overthink and ask themselves, when they see others who seem better and more attractive on social media. In addition, comparing real life with the "perfect" life shown on social media can make people feel insecure and lose their self-confidence. If someone continues to be affected by these negative influences, it can harm their mental and physical health. For example, some individuals force themselves to buy expensive items or follow lifestyle trends to gain recognition on social media. This consumptive behavior can affect their financial situation, and in some cases, push them to find quick ways to earn money, such as taking out online loans. Therefore, following social media standards without self-control can cause more negative effects than benefits.

In my opinion, in this era full of unrealistic standards, choosing to accept ourselves as we are is one of the most important things, because the standards shown on social media often do not reflect real life. What we see there is usually only a small part of someone's life, created to present an ideal self-image. As a result, many individuals feel pressured, so they keep comparing themselves to these unrealistic images. By accepting themselves, people can live more peacefully without negative effects on their mental health and can focus more on their personal growth. For example, someone who accepts both their strengths and weaknesses will be wiser when using social media. They use these standards as a way to express themselves positively and are not easily influenced by trends or the need for validation from others. Therefore, self-acceptance becomes the key to living a healthy life physically, mentally, and emotionally in an era of increasing social comparison.

In conclusion, it is important for individuals to realize that not everything shown on social media reflects reality and shouldn't be taken as a standard for life. When used wisely, social media can be a source of inspiration and opportunities in many aspects. However, if it is not used properly, it can harm a person's mental health and behavior, making them overly obsessed. Therefore, social media should be a tool, not a standard for life. People should stop forcing themselves to fit into standards created by others and start accepting themselves as they are.

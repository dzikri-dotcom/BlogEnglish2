Personal Narrative & Aspirations

"The Journey of a Clumsy Person"

If there is any person that can represent the word “clumsy,” that person is me, Julius. For me, making mistakes are part of learning in life. Some people may say that mistakes are bad for the future, but I think differently. I believe mistakes are important because they help people grow and understand themselves better. Of course, I do not mean making mistakes on purpose, but the accidental ones that happen in daily life. From those experiences, I can understand how other people think about my actions, and I can learn many new things from them. This mindset also helped me a lot in my gaming journey.

Since junior high school, I dreamed of becoming a professional player. At that time, I spent many hours every day playing games such as PUBG Mobile and Mobile Legends while still focusing on school. I joined many small online and offline tournaments for beginners and achieved several good results, such as becoming champion, runner-up, third place, and even MVP in Mobile Legends tournaments. During senior high school, I also became interested in eFootball and FIFA, although PUBG Mobile still remains my favorite game until now.

One of the most unforgettable moments in my life was when I got a trial chance for the MDL team of ONIC Esports. Unfortunately, I was eliminated in the second phase, but it became a valuable experience for me. Besides gaming, I also enjoy basketball and sports because I am a very active person. Music is also an important part of my life because it makes me feel more alive and motivated.

In conclusion, all of my hobbies, interests, and experiences have shaped me into the person I am today. Even though my dream has changed from becoming a professional player to becoming a front-end engineer, I still carry the same passion and determination in everything I do. I believe that every experience, whether success or failure, will always become an important lesson for my future. If another opportunity in esports comes someday, I will be ready to give my best effort again without forgetting my current goals and responsibilities.

The Impact of ICT on Communication and Interaction in Video Games

Gaming has come a long way from sitting alone in front of a screen trying to beat a level. Today, millions of players around the world log in every day to compete, cooperate, and connect with each other in real time. That massive shift would not have been possible without Information and Communication Technology (ICT). From the quick voice calls in PUBG Mobile to the rapid-fire pings in Mobile Legends, ICT has completely changed how players talk, plan, and bond during gameplay. While some argue that these tools have also opened the door to toxicity and conflict, ICT has, without question, made video game communication richer, faster, and more meaningful than ever before.

The most visible impact of ICT on gaming is how it enables players to communicate instantly during matches. Games like Valorant and Mobile Legends come packed with voice chat, ping systems, and quick-chat options that let teammates share information without even typing a word. In PUBG Mobile, a well-timed ping to signal an enemy position or a quick voice callout to regroup can be the difference between a squad wipe and a win throughout battles. These tools transform what could have been a disconnected multiplayer experience into something that actually requires coordination and real teamwork. ICT has essentially made communication a core gameplay mechanic, not just an optional feature sitting in the settings menu that is rarely to used.

Beyond individual matches, ICT has turned video games into genuine social spaces. Guild systems in Mobile Legends, Discord servers for Valorant squads, and live chat on various stream platforms have all created communities where players connect, share strategies, and build friendships that extend far beyond the game itself. A player in Jakarta can team up with someone in Bangkok, and through shared ICT tools, they can coordinate, laugh, and grow together across sessions. For many players, especially college students in new environments, these gaming communities become a real social outlet and support system. ICT has made gaming far more than just a hobby; it has made it a place where people genuinely belong.

That said, ICT has also made it easier for the worst parts of human behavior to show up in gaming. The anonymity that comes with online interaction often emboldens players to be toxic in ways they never would be face-to-face. For example, Mobile Legends' in-game chat often contains flaming and trash talk that can ruin the experience for newer players. Language barriers and different playstyle interpretations also lead to miscommunication, even when all the ICT tools are functioning properly. And in high-pressure ranked matches, voice chat can become chaotic noise rather than useful strategy. These are real problems that ICT alone cannot fix; they require better player behavior and stronger moderation systems from developers.

ICT has fundamentally reshaped how players interact and communicate in video games, transforming them from isolated experiences into rich, interconnected social environments. Through real-time tools in games like Valorant, Mobile Legends, and PUBG Mobile, communication has become central to how games are played and enjoyed. ICT has also fostered lasting communities and cross-cultural friendships that extend far beyond the game itself. However, it has also opened the door to toxicity, miscommunication, and pressure that can harm the overall experience. Ultimately, ICT's impact on gaming communication is undeniably transformative but maximizing its benefits still depends on how responsibly players and developers use these powerful tools.
